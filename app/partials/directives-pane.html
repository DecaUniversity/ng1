Directives are markers on a DOM element that tell AngularJS' HTML compiler ($compile) to attach specified behavior to that DOM element or even transform the element and its children.

For AngularJS, "compilation" means attaching directives to the HTML to make it interactive. It is a recursive process similar to that of compiling programming languages.

Angular goes through a process of normalizing an element's tag and attribute names when trying to match elements with directives. The process is as follows: (1) Strip the x- and data- from the front of the element/attributes, (2) Convert the :, - or _ delimited name to camelCase.
